Travel Tips to Save Money

Do you want to travel the world, but don't want to spend too much money?

Traveling the world can be easy and fun, and does not have to be expensive. These simple tips will help you think again about how to make your dreams of traveling the world a reality. Also, it won't leave a hole in your wallet! üõ©

  • Find cheaper accomodations.
    When you think about travel, you may think of resorts and fancy accomodations. Those can add up in cost, but if you want to experience more of the local culture and save money, keep reading! Staying in homestays, Air BnBs, hostels, and guesthouses, can save a lot of money. When I was in Bali, I stayed in a homestay villa, multiple ones, for less than $20 per night. All the villas had free breakfast served to me on the front balcony or patio area. Who can say no to dragon fruit and amazing Balinese coffee? On the same islands, the average resort costs were around $50-200 per night. I spent way less, and was able to travel for more time doing more fun activities like touring the natural landscapes and waterfalls on a motorbike.

  • Stay in a place with a kitchen, or at least a microvave and fridge.
    This helps to cut out eating out or dining expenses. For instance, when I was in Switzerland, the price for a meal at a fine restaurant was well over $50 per person per plate! I stayed in a hostel dorm with a kitchen. This allowed me to try to meal prep, and also fail at cooking. However, the lesson is, I met a sweet traveling couple with their kids, who offered me some of their ingredients, to make somewhat edible pasta. I also got some frozen meals. I similarly, with better luck, made some good arepas and wraps in Cartagena, Colombia at my hostel kitchen. We had communal kitchen events were travelers, who could cook better than me, made me great food. It was a 'win win' situation, and I made new friends! Shopping at local grocery stores or farmers' markets saved me so much money overall. Therefore, traveled for months.

  • Try living like a local when traveling.
    Resorts are nice and all, but you don't get the same authentic experience. Some people do not care for that, and that is fine. I have been able to find comfort, luxury, and get authentic eperiences through my travels. How? I lived like a local. I would take public transportation, like trains, buses, ferries, rideshare, over private cabs. I felt safe as a solo female traveler due to my street smarts and learning the surroundings and local languages. I ate where locals ate and loved tried street food. Of course, I took precautions with finding safe places, getting proper sanitation and vaccines. Safety first! For instance, in India, I avoided places that did not use filtered water. I gained so much value by stretching that rupee, peso, euro, etc.

  • Try using credit card and airline rewards or miles to get free flights and accomodations.
    I recommend getting one with a sign-on bonus and more points on travel purchases. Many cards, with different banks, offer travel credit or reiumbursement. I have gone on many free flights and gotten upgrades for free. I somehow ended up on Emirates first class flight to Dubai from India without actively trying. I paid nothing extra. I had enough airline miles or points to get me a free upgrade and loved it!
